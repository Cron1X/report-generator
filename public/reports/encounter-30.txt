Encounter 30 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on jobs and responsibilities, first conditional sentences, relative clauses, and talking about the past using when. The aim was to describe work clearly and connect ideas logically.

What You Practised

You practised talking about jobs and responsibilities.
Examples you practised:

I manage a team of workers.
I’m responsible for writing product descriptions.

You worked with the first conditional.
Examples:

If my head hurts, I drink a lot of water.
If it rains, I bring an umbrella.

You practised using who and which.
Examples:

We’re taking the train that leaves on Thursday evening.
I have a friend who lives in Mexico.

You practised talking about the past with when.
Examples:

When I was a child, I didn’t like vegetables.
When I was younger, I played sports.

What to Practise More

1. Building Clear Conditional Sentences

If I have time, I relax.

2. Using who and which Correctly

A friend who lives nearby.

Final Comment

This unit focused on connecting ideas about work and life. To improve further, practise conditionals and relative clauses in longer sentences.
