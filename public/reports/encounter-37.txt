Encounter 37 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on making plans, talking about future certainty using will and won’t, reported speech with said, and asking for permission using modal verbs. The unit also introduced workplace rules and discussing them clearly.

What You Practised

You worked on making plans and responding to invitations.
Examples you practised:

Let’s have a picnic on Saturday afternoon.
I can’t make it then.
I’ve gotta go now.

You practised using will and won’t to talk about things you are sure about.
Examples:

John will be late for the movie.
Sunny won’t be late for the movie.
I will be busy tomorrow.
I won’t have time this evening.

You worked on reported speech using said.
Examples:

Jack said that his friend’s name is Pete.
Jack said he likes watching movies.
Jack said he will go next week.

You practised asking for permission using different forms.
Examples:

Can I leave early today?
Am I allowed to leave early today?
May I leave early today?

You also practised rules and obligations at work.
Examples:

You must arrive on time.
You must not smoke in the office.
You have to wear a uniform.
You don’t have to work on Fridays.

You practised discussing workplace rules.
Examples:

Employees should start work early.
Employees won’t have to wear a uniform.

What to Practise More

1. Using will and won’t for Certainty

I will finish the report today.
I won’t be late tomorrow.

2. Asking for Permission Politely

May I leave early today?
Can I take a longer lunch break?

Final Comment

This unit focused on future certainty, permission, and workplace rules. To improve further, practise will and won’t confidently and vary permission questions in full sentences.
