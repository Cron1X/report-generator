Encounter 53 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on job applications, responsibilities at work, explaining plans that did not happen, and contrasting ideas using linking expressions. The unit also developed discussion skills around job conditions and workplace expectations.

What You Practised

You worked on job-related vocabulary and recruitment language.
Examples you practised:

apply for a job
send a resume
have an interview
receive a job offer

You practised talking about responsibilities at work.
Examples:

It’s my responsibility to prepare food.
I’m responsible for preparing food.
My responsibilities include preparing food.
It’s up to me to order supplies.

You practised explaining duties in different jobs.
Examples:

I train new waiters.
I take reservations.
I prepare the dining room for guests.
I clean tables.
I supervise the waiters.

You worked on explaining plans that didn’t happen using was going to, was planning to, and was hoping to.
Examples:

I was going to phone my colleague, but he didn’t answer.
I was planning to create the presentation, but my computer crashed.
I was hoping to start writing the report, but I didn’t have time.

You practised linking contrasting ideas using even though.
Examples:

Even though my boss offered me more money, I still quit my job.
I still quit my job even though my boss offered me more money.

You also discussed job conditions and preferences.
Examples:

Salary and working hours are important.
Enjoyment and commute time matter to me.

What to Practise More

1. Responsibility Language

Practise switching between different forms smoothly.

I’m responsible for…
My responsibilities include…

2. Explaining Missed Plans Clearly

Use but to show contrast.

I was going to…, but…

Final Comment

This unit focused on work, responsibility, and explaining outcomes. To improve further, practise responsibility structures and contrasting ideas in longer, clearer sentences.
