Encounter 64 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on talking about upcoming events, giving approximate information, reported speech, and explaining expectations that were not met. The unit also developed polite questioning and clarification skills.

What You Practised

You worked on talking about events with limited information.
Examples you practised:

It’s open till around 10:00.
It’s open from noon until about 10:00.
There will be pizza, pasta, and stuff like that.

You practised asking questions about events.
Examples:

When is it opening?
Where is it being held?
Who is being featured?
What kind of art will be shown?

You worked on more formal future expressions.
Examples:

The restaurant is set to open next month.
The movie will be released next month.
Classes for the next semester begin September 12th.

You practised reported speech using said and told.
Examples:

Last week you said the new restaurant was opening soon.
You told me the movie was very long.

You practised talking about expectations using was supposed to.
Examples:

The restaurant opening was supposed to be October 10th.
He was supposed to arrive at 5:00, but he was late.

You practised explaining problems and reasons.
Examples:

She was worried she hadn’t advertised enough.
There were problems with the electricity.

You practised handling complaints politely.
Examples:

I told you we would arrive at 6:00 p.m.
I told you we would like a table with a nice view.

What to Practise More

1. Reported Speech Accuracy

Pay attention to tense changes after said and told.

2. Explaining Expectations

Use was supposed to clearly with a contrasting result.

Final Comment

This unit focused on events, expectations, and clarification. To improve further, practise reported speech and explaining problems in a calm, structured way.
