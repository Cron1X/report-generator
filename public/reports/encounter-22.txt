Encounter 22 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on school subjects, school rules, and expressing obligation and necessity using must, must not, and have to. The unit also introduced giving reasons with because and so, and expressing opinions using I think.

What You Practised

You worked with school subjects and basic opinions about them.
Examples you practised:

math
chemistry
history
biology

It’s math.
Students learn about history.

You practised talking about school rules using must and must not.
Examples:

Students must come to class on time.
Students must not eat in class.
Students must bring their books to class.

You also practised using have to to talk about rules and responsibilities.
Examples:

You have to come to class on time.
You have to do your homework on time.

You compared must not and don’t have to to show different meanings.
Examples:

Students must not eat in class.
Students don’t have to eat in class.

You practised giving reasons using because and so.
Examples:

Good students pay attention in class because they want to learn.
They want to learn, so they pay attention in class.

You also practised giving opinions using I think.
Examples:

I think it’s interesting.
I think it’s fun.

What to Practise More

1. Using must and have to Clearly

Students must pay attention in class.
You have to do your homework on time.

2. Giving Clear Reasons

Good students learn a lot because they study.
They study hard, so they do well on tests.

Final Comment

This unit focused on school-related language, rules, and giving reasons. To improve further, practise using must and have to accurately and linking ideas clearly with because and so.
