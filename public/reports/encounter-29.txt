Encounter 29 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on invitations, making excuses, past ability using could and couldn’t, and quantity expressions with too and enough. The aim was to make plans politely and explain why something was not possible.

What You Practised

You practised inviting and responding politely.
Examples you practised:

Do you want to come to a party?
I’m afraid I’m busy.
What about a different time?

You practised accepting and refusing invitations.
Examples:

Sure.
Unfortunately, I can’t go.
Thank you for the invitation.

You worked with could and couldn’t in the past.
Examples:

When I was 10, I couldn’t speak English.
When I was 8, I could swim.

You practised too and enough.
Examples:

It was too hot.
I didn’t have enough money.
There aren’t enough things to do.

You also practised making excuses.
Examples:

I couldn’t call you because I was busy.

What to Practise More

1. Explaining Reasons Clearly

I couldn’t go because I was busy.

2. Using too vs enough

Too tired
Not enough time

Final Comment

This unit focused on polite communication and giving reasons. To improve further, practise invitations, excuses, and quantity language in full sentences.
