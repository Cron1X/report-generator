Encounter 4 – Student Feedback Report

In this unit, we focused on talking about jobs, identifying everyday objects, describing them clearly, and showing possession using the genitive ’s. You participated well and showed good understanding of how to talk about people and their belongings in simple, clear sentences.

You practiced talking about jobs using I’m a / I work as.
Examples:
- I’m a teacher.
- I’m a student.
- I’m a cook.
- I work as a driver.

You also practiced identifying common objects.
Examples:
- It’s a bag.
- It’s a phone.
- It’s a wallet.
- They’re keys.
- They’re glasses.

A key focus of this unit was showing possession using the genitive ’s.
Examples:
- It’s Emma’s bag.
- It’s John’s phone.
- It’s the teacher’s book.
- It’s the student’s desk.
- Whose bag is this? It’s Anna’s bag.

You were able to answer Whose…? questions accurately in most cases and showed good awareness of who an object belongs to.

You also practiced describing objects using simple adjectives.
Examples:
- It’s a new phone.
- It’s an old bag.
- It’s a big wallet.
- It’s a small key.
- It’s clean.
- It’s dirty.

You were generally able to combine description and possession.
Examples:
- It’s Anna’s new phone.
- It’s John’s old bag.

To continue improving, focus on:
- Pronouncing the ’s ending clearly
- Using full sentences instead of short answers
- Combining possession and description more naturally

Overall, this unit helped you speak more clearly about people, their jobs, and their belongings. With continued practice, your accuracy and confidence will continue to improve.
