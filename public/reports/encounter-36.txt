Encounter 36 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on crime-related vocabulary, expressing opinions, agreeing and disagreeing, and linking ideas using however.

What You Practised

You worked with crime vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

a thief
a criminal
steal
rob
robbery

He was arrested for stealing money.

You practised expressing opinions.
Examples:

I think he’s guilty.
I don’t think he’s guilty.
I believe that he stole the money.

You practised agreeing and disagreeing.
Examples:

I agree with you.
I don’t agree with you.
I disagree.

You worked with fact vs opinion.
Examples:

He’s guilty.
I think he’s guilty.

You practised linking contrasting ideas using however.
Examples:

Crime isn’t a big problem in this city; however, in some cities it is serious.
A big city is better to live in; however, there is more crime.

You practised discussing opinions and asking why.
Examples:

What do you think?
Why do you say so?

What to Practise More

1. Using Opinion Phrases Clearly

I think that…
I believe that…

2. Linking Ideas with however

It’s a good city; however, it is expensive.

Final Comment

This unit focused on opinions, agreement, and contrasting ideas. To improve further, practise expressing opinions clearly and linking ideas smoothly using however.
