Encounter 9 – Student Feedback Report

In this unit, we focused on checking information, using It’s… to talk about time, weather, and situations, forming yes/no questions with be, do, and can, giving reasons using because, and spelling words clearly. You participated actively and showed good engagement throughout the lesson.

You practiced checking information and responding clearly.
Examples:
- It’s true.
- It’s false.
- No, that’s not correct.

You worked on using It’s… to describe different situations.
Examples:
- It’s hot.
- It’s cold.
- It’s windy.
- It’s two o’clock.
- It’s difficult.

You practiced asking and answering yes/no questions using be, do, and can.
Examples:
- Are you married?
- Yes, I am. / No, I’m not.
- Do you like coffee?
- Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.
- Can you drive?
- Yes, I can. / No, I can’t.

Giving reasons using because was an important focus.
Examples:
- I don’t like cats because I’m scared.
- We can’t go because it’s raining.
- I like summer because it’s warm.

You also practiced spelling words and asking for clarification politely.
Examples:
- Can you spell it, please?
- Can you repeat that, please?
- B-R-E-A-K-F-A-S-T.

During the speaking activities, you responded confidently and were able to correct information when needed. At times, you could add more detail to your answers, but your participation was positive.

To continue improving, focus on:
- Extending answers with one more idea
- Using because more naturally in conversation
- Spelling longer words smoothly

Overall, this unit helped strengthen your basic communication skills and your ability to explain simple ideas clearly. With continued practice, your fluency and confidence will continue to improve.
