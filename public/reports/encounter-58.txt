Encounter 58 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on computer security, making polite requests and refusals, comparing items using both / neither, and handling customer service situations. The unit developed language for persuasion, comparison, and problem-solving.

What You Practised

You worked on vocabulary related to computer problems and security.
Examples you practised:

virus
spam emails
hacking
virus protection software

Example sentences:

It had a virus.
You can run a virus protection program.
Viruses can come through spam emails.

You practised making polite requests and asking for help.
Examples:

Can you help me by lending me your mobile phone?
I’d be grateful if you could help.
I’d really appreciate it if you could lend me your phone.

You also practised informal refusals and acceptance language.
Examples:

Whatever. Take it.
Okay, I give in.
All right.

You worked on comparison structures using both and neither.
Examples:

Both Walter and Flynn are boys.
Neither Walter nor Flynn likes baths.
Both computers weigh three kilograms.
Neither computer is light.

You practised describing similarities between products.
Examples:

Both computers have long battery life.
Neither phone is cheap.

You practised customer service language and complaints.
Examples:

There must be a solution.
I’m sorry, but I can’t give your money back.
I’d be grateful if you could help.

What to Practise More

1. Polite Request Variety

Practise switching between can you, could you, and I’d appreciate it if…

2. Comparison Accuracy

Pay attention to verb agreement after both and neither.

Final Comment

This unit focused on practical communication, comparison, and problem-solving. To improve further, practise polite requests and comparison sentences in longer, more detailed explanations.
