Encounter 69 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on expressing degrees of certainty, making deductions about the past, and reporting what other people said. The unit developed narrative and interpretation skills.

What You Practised

You worked on certainty and probability language.
Examples practised:

She might have been a good student.
She must have been a good student.
I’m not sure she was a good student.

You practised explaining reasons for decisions.
Examples:

She left the company to start her own business.
They had disagreements about how to run the company.

You worked on reported speech.
Examples:

Khae said she couldn’t believe Marco wanted to sell the company.
You said you were hungry.

You practised past habits and contrasts.
Examples:

Before, they wouldn’t go out because they were broke.
In the past, they would spend more time together.

You practised storytelling and asking follow-up questions.
Examples:

What advice did they give you?
How did it help you?

What to Practise More

Certainty Language
Practise the difference between might have and must have.

Reported Speech Accuracy
Pay attention to tense changes after said.

Final Comment

This unit focused on interpreting situations and telling stories. To improve further, practise giving clear reasons and using reported speech naturally.
