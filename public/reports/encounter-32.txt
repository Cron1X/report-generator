In this lesson, we focused on talking about illness and symptoms, giving advice using can, asking and answering questions about health, and using comparative adjectives. The unit also introduced question words to gather information clearly in conversations.

What You Practised

You worked with illness and symptom vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

He had a fever.
He felt weak.
He had a runny nose.
They felt very sick.
She had a stomachache.

You practised using this language to describe how someone feels.
Examples:

He felt ill and had to lie down.
She felt sick and needed to rest.

You worked with advice and possible actions using can.
Examples:

He can take some medicine.
He can lie down.
He can rest.
He can go to the doctor.
He can drink lots of water.

You also practised adjectives and comparative forms.
Examples:

Benny is tall.
Shen is intelligent.
Benny is taller than Shen.
Shen is more intelligent than Benny.

You practised question words to ask for information.
Examples:

Who felt ill and went to the doctor?
Where are the employees waiting?
What is Rima looking for?
Which way do I go?
What is the name of the street?

You practised using which to make choices.
Examples:

Which do you want for dinner?
