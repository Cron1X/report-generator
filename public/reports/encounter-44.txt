Encounter 44 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on television programs, storytelling, and sequencing events clearly. The unit also developed opinion language and narrative connectors.

What You Practised

You worked on types of TV shows.
Examples you practised:

reality shows
documentaries
news programs
entertainment shows

You practised describing stories using key elements.
Examples:

the characters
the setting
the plot
the genre

You worked on sequencing events in stories.
Examples:

After he met Sean, he planned his escape.
Eventually, he proved he was innocent.
In the end, the story finishes happily.

You practised narrative time expressions.
Examples:

during
until
in the present
at the end

You also practised giving opinions about TV and films.
Examples:

I prefer films because they have more variety.
TV series are longer, so the story has more detail.

What to Practise More

1. Story Sequencing

Use linking words to show order clearly.

After that…
Eventually…
In the end…

2. Giving Clear Opinions

Use because to explain your reasons.

Final Comment

This unit focused on storytelling and expressing opinions. To improve further, practise sequencing events smoothly and supporting opinions with clear reasons.
