Encounter 60 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on giving advice, expressing sympathy, checking details using question tags, and agreeing with opinions. The unit developed travel-related communication and reassurance language.

What You Practised

You worked on expressing sympathy and encouragement.
Examples you practised:

Cheer up!
Don’t worry! It’ll be fine.

You practised giving advice using different structures.
Examples:

You’d better go to the art museum.
You shouldn’t miss it.
If I were you, I’d walk around the city center.

You practised asking for and giving travel advice.
Examples:

Can you recommend a place to eat?
Where should I go?

You worked on question tags to confirm information.
Examples:

You’re arriving at 10:00 a.m., aren’t you?
You’re staying at the hotel, aren’t you?

You practised agreeing strongly with opinions.
Examples:

I completely agree.
You’re right about that.
I’m of the same opinion.

You practised organizing plans and solving last-minute problems.
Examples:

You should take the subway to the airport.
You ought to leave early.

What to Practise More

1. Advice Structures

Practise switching between should, ought to, and if I were you.

2. Question Tags

Check verb forms and intonation.

Final Comment

This unit focused on advice, reassurance, and planning. To improve further, practise combining advice with clear explanations.
