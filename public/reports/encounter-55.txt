Encounter 55 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on advertising, marketing, persuasion, and describing target markets. The unit also developed language for influencing people and excluding exceptions.

What You Practised

You worked on marketing and advertising vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

advertise
promote a product
target market
ad campaign
TV commercial
online advertising

You practised describing ways to promote a business.
Examples:

She can tell friends.
She can set up a Facebook page.
She can hand out flyers.
She can advertise in the local newspaper.

You practised talking about groups and age ranges.
Examples:

teenagers (13–19)
middle-aged people (40–60)
adults
elderly people

You worked on persuasion structures using get someone to.
Examples:

We got them to buy cell phones.
The CEO got Marco to finish the presentation.

You practised exceptions using except for.
Examples:

Everyone likes cake except for Tom.
All my friends are married except for me.

You practised planning and explaining advertising strategies.
Examples:

First, we choose the target market.
Next, we decide on advertising.
Then, we launch the campaign.

What to Practise More

1. Persuasion Language

Practise get someone to in different contexts.

2. Clear Planning Language

Use sequence words: first, next, then, finally.

Final Comment

This unit focused on marketing and persuasion. To improve further, practise explaining plans step by step and using persuasion structures confidently.
