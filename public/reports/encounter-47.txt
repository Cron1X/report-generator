Encounter 47 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on food and drink vocabulary, describing tastes, making suggestions, comparing choices, and linking ideas naturally using reference words and indirect questions. The unit also developed communication skills for planning meals and deciding where to eat.

What You Practised

You worked on food and drink vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

lobster
cabbage
milk
tea
cola
sparkling water

You practised describing taste.
Examples:

It looks delicious.
It tastes sour.
It tastes bitter.
It’s spicy.
It tastes bad.

You worked on describing drinks and temperature.
Examples:

Cold drinks
Hot drinks
Fruit juice
Still water
Sparkling water

You practised making suggestions and responding.
Examples:

Why don’t we go to a restaurant instead?
Yeah, sure.
I think we should eat out.

You practised comparing options.
Examples:

It’s cheaper and faster than going to a restaurant.
Riding bikes is healthier than taking a taxi.
Taking a taxi is faster than riding bikes.

You worked on linking ideas naturally.
Examples:

That’s why I make lunch at home.
That’s where I sit in the evenings.
That was when I realized I wanted to be an actor.

You practised indirect questions.
Examples:

Could you tell me where to get lunch?
Could you tell me how to get there?

What to Practise More

1. Describing Food Clearly

Use taste adjectives confidently.

It tastes sour.
It’s spicy and hot.

2. Linking Sentences Naturally

Use that’s why, that’s where, that’s when to avoid repetition.

Final Comment

This unit focused on food, comparison, and connected speech. To improve further, practise taste descriptions and linking ideas smoothly when explaining choices.
