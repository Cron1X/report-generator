Encounter 43 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on describing objects, using the passive voice, and making comparisons. The unit also developed detailed descriptions using size, material, condition, and degree of difference.

What You Practised

You worked on describing objects using physical features.
Examples you practised:

It’s round.
It’s made of wood.
It’s heavy.
It’s light.
It’s in good condition.

You practised using the passive voice.
Examples:

The first mobile phone was made by Motorola.
It was made in 1979.
This book was written by a famous author.

You worked on comparisons using bigger than, smaller than, and modifiers.
Examples:

The first mobile phone was much bigger than modern phones.
This phone is slightly smaller than that one.
This one is a lot heavier.

You practised giving detailed comparisons.
Examples:

This city is much busier than my hometown.
My life now is very different from ten years ago.

You also practised reacting naturally in conversation.
Examples:

Really?
No way!
You’re kidding!
I can’t believe that!

What to Practise More

1. Passive Voice Questions

When was it made?
Where was it made?
Who was it made by?

2. Using Modifiers in Comparisons

much bigger
a bit smaller
a lot more expensive

Final Comment

This unit focused on describing objects clearly and comparing them in detail. To improve further, practise passive structures and adding more descriptive detail when comparing things.
