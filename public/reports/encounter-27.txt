Encounter 27 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on food and meals, quantity language, daily routines, and common phrasal verbs. The aim was to talk about what people eat, how much they eat, and describe everyday actions in a clear and connected way.

What You Practised

You worked with food and meal vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

breakfast
lunch
dinner
snacks

I eat breakfast in the morning.
I have lunch at work.
I eat dinner in the evening.

You practised talking about quantities using much and many.
Examples:

too much water
too many glasses of water

You drink too much water every day.
You drink too many glasses of water every day.

You also worked with enough.
Examples:

It’s not enough water.
There isn’t enough food.

You practised sequencing daily actions using linking words.
Examples:

First, I get up.
Then, I have a shower.
After that, I eat breakfast.

You worked with common phrasal verbs.
Examples:

wake up
get up
sit down
go out
calm down
slow down

You also practised talking about healthy and unhealthy diets.
Examples:

You eat too much sugar.
You should eat more vegetables.

What to Practise More

1. Choosing much and many Correctly

Too much water
Too many snacks

2. Connecting Ideas Smoothly

First I wake up, then I eat breakfast.

Final Comment

This unit focused on food, quantity, and daily routines. To improve further, practise much, many, and enough, and focus on linking actions smoothly when describing your day.
