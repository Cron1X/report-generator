Encounter 66 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on friendships, invitations, complaints, regret, and giving advice. The unit also developed emotional language and reflective discussion skills.

What You Practised

You worked on describing friendships.
Examples you practised:

close friends
friends from school
inseparable
lose touch

You practised inviting and refusing politely.
Examples:

I’d like to, but I might be busy.
I don’t know if I can.
I’m not sure; I’ll see what I can do.

You practised expressing frustration and complaints.
Examples:

I’ve had enough.
I’m sick of my boss.
That won’t make any difference.

You worked on expressing regret.
Examples:

I shouldn’t have been rude.
I wish I hadn’t said that.
I wish I had travelled more when I was young.

You practised giving advice and responding.
Examples:

You should take time off work.
There’s nothing I can do.

You practised discussing opinions about friendship.
Examples:

It takes a lot of effort to make a friendship work.
Good friends sometimes disagree.

What to Practise More

1. Regret Structures

Practise should have and wish I had with clear situations.

2. Polite Refusals

Vary refusal language to sound natural.

Final Comment

This unit focused on relationships and reflection. To improve further, practise expressing regret clearly and balancing advice with empathy.
