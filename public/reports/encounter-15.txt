Encounter 15 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on describing places in town, giving directions, and using prepositions of place. The aim was to help describe locations clearly and explain how to get from one place to another.

What You Practised

You worked with common places in town.
Examples you practised:

bank, supermarket, hospital, school, café, restaurant

The bank is next to the supermarket.
The café is near the station.

You practised prepositions of place.
Examples:

next to
across from
behind
in front of
between

The bank is next to the supermarket.
The café is across from the school.

You also practised giving simple directions.
Examples:

Go straight ahead.
Turn left.
Turn right at the corner.
It’s near the station.

You practised asking about locations.
Examples:

Where is the bank?
Is there a café near here?
How do I get to the station?

What to Practise More

1. Using Prepositions Accurately

Focus on choosing the correct preposition.

The bank is next to the supermarket.
The café is across from the school.

2. Giving Directions in Full Sentences

Go straight ahead and turn left.
It’s near the station, next to the bank.

Final Comment

This unit focused on describing places and giving directions clearly. To improve further, practise using prepositions confidently and giving directions using full sentences.
