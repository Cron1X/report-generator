Encounter 67 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on technology, advantages and disadvantages, expressing strong emotions, drawing conclusions, and giving advice. The unit also developed discussion and problem-solving language.

What You Practised

You worked on technology vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

laptop
tablet
mobile phone
wireless Internet
search engines
social networking

You practised discussing advantages and disadvantages.
Examples:

A laptop is better because it has a bigger screen.
The disadvantage of a tablet is that it’s difficult to hold.
Social networking has many drawbacks.

You practised expressing anger and denying blame.
Examples:

I’m furious.
It’s not my fault.
I’m not to blame.

You worked on drawing conclusions.
Examples:

I can only conclude that the store sells low-quality products.
Therefore, the product is unreliable.

You practised giving suggestions and offers of help.
Examples:

You could take it back to the shop.
Shall I search online for you?

You practised discussing technology and social impact.
Examples:

People spend more time online and less time with real people.
Technology helps us learn faster.

What to Practise More

1. Conclusion Language

Use therefore and as a result carefully.

2. Balanced Arguments

Practise giving both advantages and disadvantages clearly.

Final Comment

This unit focused on technology, emotion, and reasoning. To improve further, practise structuring arguments clearly and supporting opinions with examples.
