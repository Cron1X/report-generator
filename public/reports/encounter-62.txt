Encounter 62 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on job interviews, describing strengths and weaknesses, conditional rules, and giving career advice. The unit developed professional communication skills.

What You Practised

You worked on job-related adjectives.
Examples you practised:

rewarding
fulfilling
dead-end
low-paid

You practised interview language.
Examples:

What experience do you have?
What are your strengths and weaknesses?

You practised describing experience and personality.
Examples:

I have ten years of management experience.
I’m a good listener, but I can lack patience.

You worked on hiring and evaluation language.
Examples:

The ideal candidate should have relevant experience.
She has a strong track record.

You practised conditional rules using only if, unless, and even if.
Examples:

You’ll get another interview only if you gain experience.
I won’t hire you unless you improve your skills.

You practised giving career advice.
Examples:

My advice is to find a stable career.
I’d tell her to choose a job she enjoys.

What to Practise More

1. Interview Structure

Practise organizing answers clearly.

2. Conditional Accuracy
