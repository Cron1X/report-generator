Encounter 56 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on environmental issues, cause-and-effect language, expressing concern, and agreeing or disagreeing politely. The unit also developed discussion skills around global problems.

What You Practised

You worked on environmental vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

drought
flood
pollution
climate change
water shortages

You practised expressing concern.
Examples:

I’m worried about pollution.
I’m worried that pollution is going to get worse.

You worked on cause-and-effect structures.
Examples:

There were floods because of heavy rain.
The floods were caused by heavy rain.
As a result of heavy rain, there were floods.

You practised linking causes and results.
Examples:

There was no rain, so there was a drought.
The drought was caused by a lack of rain.

You practised agreeing and disagreeing politely.
Examples:

I think so, too.
I don’t think that’s true.
Yes, but…

You practised discussing pros and cons.
Examples:

A good point is less pollution.
A bad point is inconvenient travel.

What to Practise More

1. Cause-and-Effect Variety

Avoid repeating because by using different structures.

2. Polite Disagreement

Use soft language when disagreeing.

Final Comment

This unit focused on environmental issues and reasoning. To improve further, practise varied cause-and-effect expressions and balanced discussion language.
