Encounter 70 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on agreeing and disagreeing at work, making polite requests, and discussing business decisions. The unit developed negotiation and collaboration language.

What You Practised

You worked on expressing agreement and disagreement.
Examples practised:

Absolutely.
I don’t really care.
I don’t mind either way.

You practised workplace requests.
Examples:

Could you possibly work late this evening?
Would you mind sending the report?
I was wondering if you could help train a new employee.

You worked on comparing business options.
Examples:

We could open a shop, but it might be expensive.
Although it’s a smaller market, it could be more profitable.

You practised checking details.
Examples:

You called the staff yesterday, didn’t you?
Perhaps now is a good time to do it?

What to Practise More

Polite Request Forms
Practise longer, indirect request structures.

Decision Language
Use although and on the other hand to balance ideas.

Final Comment

This unit focused on professional collaboration. To improve further, practise making suggestions and responding diplomatically.
