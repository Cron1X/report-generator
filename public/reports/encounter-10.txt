Encounter 10 – Student Feedback Report

In this unit, we focused on days and months, prepositions of time and place, using have to and need to, and talking about routines and schedules. You participated well and showed a good understanding of how to talk about time and daily activities.

You practiced identifying days and months clearly.
Examples:
- It’s Monday.
- It’s March.
- January is the first month of the year.
- Saturday and Sunday are the weekend.

You worked on prepositions of time.
Examples:
- in the morning
- in March
- on Monday
- on my birthday
- at eight o’clock
- at lunchtime

You also practiced prepositions of place.
Examples:
- on the table
- next to the chair
- behind the desk
- in the room

Using have to and need to was an important focus when talking about responsibilities and schedules.
Examples:
- I have to work on Monday.
- I need to study tomorrow.
- I have to meet my friend in the afternoon.

You practiced talking about daily routines.
Examples:
- I wake up at seven.
- I work in the morning.
- I have lunch at one o’clock.
- I go to bed at eleven.

During the communication activities, you were able to talk about your schedule and answer questions clearly. At times, you needed a little extra time to choose the correct preposition, but your effort and participation were positive.

To continue improving, focus on:
- Choosing in, on, and at more quickly
- Using full sentences when talking about routines
- Adding time expressions to your answers

Overall, this unit helped you speak more confidently about time, routines, and daily responsibilities. With continued practice, your fluency and accuracy will continue to improve.
