Encounter 8 – Student Feedback Report

In this unit, we focused on talking about dates and times, expressing likes and dislikes, forming yes/no questions, and using can and can’t to talk about ability. You participated well and showed good engagement with the lesson activities.

You practiced saying and understanding dates and times.
Examples:
- What’s the date?
- It’s July 7th.
- What time is it?
- It’s nine o’clock.
- The meeting is at ten thirty.

You worked on talking about likes and dislikes using simple, clear sentences.
Examples:
- I like movies.
- I like basketball.
- I don’t like singing.
- I don’t like coffee.
- Do you like reading?

You practiced forming and answering yes/no questions correctly.
Examples:
- Are you Canadian?
- Yes, I am. / No, I’m not.
- Do you live in the city?
- Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.

Using can and can’t to talk about ability was an important focus.
Examples:
- I can swim.
- I can cook.
- I can’t drive.
- I can’t sing.
- Can you play football?

During the communication activities, you were able to answer personal questions and share information about yourself. You generally used full sentences, although at times you needed a little extra time to think before responding.

To continue improving, focus on:
- Answering questions more quickly and confidently
- Using full sentences consistently
- Choosing can or can’t without hesitation

Overall, this unit helped you talk more clearly about preferences, abilities, and basic personal information. With continued practice, your confidence and fluency will continue to grow.
