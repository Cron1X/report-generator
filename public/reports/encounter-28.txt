Encounter 28 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on jobs, workplaces, rules, and asking for permission using have to, don’t have to, can, and can’t. The aim was to talk about responsibilities at work and everyday rules.

What You Practised

You worked with job and workplace vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

manager
chef
designer
teacher
doctor

She works in an office.
He’s a chef.

You practised talking about rules and responsibilities.
Examples:

You have to come to work on time.
Chefs have to cook food.
Chefs don’t have to serve customers.

You practised asking for and giving permission.
Examples:

Can I start work at 9:30?
Yes, you can.
No, you can’t. You have to start at 8:30.

You also practised describing jobs and duties.
Examples:

I’m responsible for writing product descriptions.
I manage a team of workers.

What to Practise More

1. Using have to vs don’t have to

You have to wear a uniform.
You don’t have to work on Sunday.

2. Asking Politely for Permission

Can I leave work early?

Final Comment

This unit focused on work, rules, and permission. To improve further, practise have to forms and polite permission questions in full sentences.
