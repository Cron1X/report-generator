Encounter 18 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on preferences and comparisons using comparative adjectives.

What You Practised

You practised talking about preferences.
Examples:

I like coffee.
I prefer tea.

You practised comparative forms.
Examples:

This restaurant is cheaper.
This place is better.
My house is bigger than yours.

You practised making comparisons.
Examples:

Coffee is cheaper than tea.
This phone is better than the old one.

What to Practise More

1. Using Comparatives Correctly

Cheaper than
Bigger than
Better than

2. Explaining Preferences

I prefer coffee because it’s cheaper.

Final Comment

This unit focused on expressing preferences and comparisons. To improve further, practise comparative forms and explaining your choices clearly.
