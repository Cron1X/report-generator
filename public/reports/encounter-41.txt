Encounter 41 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on shopping, refunds, describing products using like, and asking for help politely. The unit also included role-play for customer and shop assistant situations.

What You Practised

You worked on shopping and return language.
Examples you practised:

You can ask for a refund.
You can take it back to the store.
You need a receipt.

You practised asking for help in shops.
Examples:

Can I help you?
I’m just looking, thanks.
Can I try it on?

You worked on describing things using like.
Examples:

What is the shop like?
What is your hometown like?
What was the movie like?

You practised explaining problems with purchases.
Examples:

It doesn’t work.
It’s too small.
It’s poorly made.

You practised asking for exchanges and refunds.
Examples:

I’d like to return this.
Can I exchange it for a smaller one?

What to Practise More

1. Using like for Descriptions

What is the restaurant like?

2. Explaining Problems Clearly

It doesn’t work properly.
It’s too big for me.

Final Comment

This unit focused on shopping, descriptions, and polite problem-solving. To improve further, practise explaining issues clearly and using like to describe places and products.
