Encounter 46 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on complaints, describing clothes, offering help, and using second conditional sentences to talk about imaginary situations.

What You Practised

You worked on complaint language.
Examples you practised:

I’d like to complain about the service.
The shop employee was rude.
Your advertisement said everything was on sale.

You practised describing clothes.
Examples:

He’s wearing a white shirt.
He has a stylish black suit.
The jacket is too big.
The pants are too tight.

You worked on offers and suggestions.
Examples:

Shall I get some milk?
I’ll get some milk.

You practised second conditional sentences.
Examples:

If I had enough money, I’d buy lunch.
If I were you, I’d complain to the manager.

You also practised problem-solving in shopping situations.
Examples:

I’d take it back to the shop.
I’d ask for a refund.

What to Practise More

1. Second Conditional Accuracy

If I had more time, I’d go shopping.

2. Polite Complaints

Use calm, clear language when complaining.

Final Comment

This unit focused on complaints, clothing, and hypothetical situations. To improve further, practise second conditional sentences and polite problem-solving language.
