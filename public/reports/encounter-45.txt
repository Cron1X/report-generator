Encounter 45 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on books and movies, giving opinions, present perfect questions, agreement phrases, and giving advice using If I were you.

What You Practised

You worked on describing books and movies.
Examples you practised:

It’s about…
It was directed by…
It was written by…
It’s action-packed.
It was gripping.

You practised asking about recent experiences using the present perfect.
Examples:

Have you watched a movie recently?
Have you seen any good movies lately?

You worked on agreement and disagreement.
Examples:

So do I.
Me too.
Neither do I.
Me neither.

You practised giving advice.
Examples:

If I were you, I’d reserve a ticket.
If I were you, I’d choose a less popular movie.

What to Practise More

1. Present Perfect Follow-Up Questions

What was it called?
When did you see it?

2. Advice Structures

If I were you, I’d…

Final Comment

This unit focused on media, experiences, and giving advice. To improve further, practise present perfect questions and expanding opinions with reasons.
