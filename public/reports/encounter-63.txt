Encounter 63 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on comparing cities, discussing advantages and disadvantages, describing population trends, and expressing opinions about urban living. The unit also developed language for statistics, proportions, and persuasion.

What You Practised

You worked on comparative and descriptive language for cities.
Examples you practised:

It has better amenities than most cities I’ve been to.
It’s bigger and more crowded than other cities.
It’s more exciting to live in, but it also has more problems with pollution and smog.

You practised describing positive and negative features of cities.
Examples:

There’s a great nightlife.
It’s overcrowded and stressful.
It has good parks and good restaurants.

You worked on population and trend language.
Examples:

The population has been increasing steadily.
Population growth shows a clear upward trend.
The chart shows a downward trend in population.

You practised ratios and proportions.
Examples:

There are two children for every one adult.
The ratio of children to adults is 2 to 1.
About 65% of the population is children.
The proportion of children in the population is 65%.

You practised balancing arguments.
Examples:

On the one hand, large cities have good employment opportunities.
On the other hand, they are often overcrowded and dirty.

You worked on superlative structures and comparisons.
Examples:

Mexico City is one of the largest cities in the world.
This job is better than almost any other job I’ve had.

You practised persuading others in discussions.
Examples:

You should move there because it’s one of the most exciting cities in the world.
There are many benefits to living there, for example…

What to Practise More

1. Trend Vocabulary

Practise using rise, fall, increase, decrease, steady, and sharp in full sentences.

2. Balanced Opinions

Link advantages and disadvantages clearly using on the one hand / on the other hand.

Final Comment

This unit focused on city life, trends, and persuasion. To improve further, practise combining statistics with clear opinions and structured arguments.
