Encounter 78 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on talking about movies, making predictions about the future, expressing degrees of certainty, and comparing people and choices using contrast. The unit developed opinion, prediction, and evaluative language.

What You Practised

You worked on expressing certainty and uncertainty about future events.
Examples practised:

I’m absolutely sure that he’ll be successful.
There’s no doubt that the movie will be popular.
I have a feeling that it will do well.
He might move to LA.
I may be wrong, but I think it won’t work.
I rather doubt that the project will succeed.

You practised talking about consequences and predictions.
Examples:

If he’s successful, he’ll move to LA.
If the movie does well, it will make more money.
The consequences could be very positive.

You worked on movie-related vocabulary and reviews.
Examples:

The review was positive.
The movie describes the origins of the story.
The acting was excellent.
The plot was exciting but unrealistic.

You practised comparing people and options using contrast.
Examples:

Although Gao’s salary is higher, he’s more popular.
Even if the plot was unrealistic, the action was fun to watch.
He may be difficult to work with, but his ability makes up for it.

You practised expressing preferences and opinions about movies.
Examples:

This was the only movie that made me laugh and cry.
It’s one of the best movies I’ve ever seen.

What to Practise More

Certainty Phrases
Practise choosing phrases that match how sure you really are.

Contrast Structures
Use although, even if, and but accurately in longer sentences.

Final Comment

This unit focused on opinions, predictions, and evaluation. To improve further, practise explaining your ideas in more detail and clearly linking causes and consequences.
