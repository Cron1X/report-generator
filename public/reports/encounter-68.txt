ncounter 68 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on expressing opinions clearly, agreeing and disagreeing, making polite requests at work, and comparing different ways of working. The unit developed language for workplace communication and decision-making.

What You Practised

You worked on expressing strong and neutral opinions.
Examples practised:

You can say that again.
Absolutely.
I don’t really mind.
I don’t have an opinion one way or the other.

You practised responding to workplace opinions.
Examples:

Working at home is more convenient than working in an office.
I don’t really agree with that.
I think that’s absolutely right.

You worked on making polite workplace requests.
Examples:

I was thinking maybe you could work late this evening.
Perhaps you can help me finish the report.
Do you think you could send me the sales figures?

You practised comparing advantages and disadvantages.
Examples:

Working at home is more convenient. On the other hand, it can be boring.
Although working in an office is more common, working from home is becoming more popular.

You worked on tag questions to check information.
Examples:

It’s a large company, isn’t it?
You were out of the office yesterday, right?

What to Practise More

Softening Requests
Practise using perhaps, maybe, and I was thinking to sound polite.

Structuring Opinions
Link ideas using although, while, and on the other hand.

Final Comment

This unit focused on professional communication. To improve further, practise expressing opinions clearly while keeping your tone polite and balanced.
