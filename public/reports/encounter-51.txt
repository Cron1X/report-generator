Encounter 51 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on preferences, describing people, relative clauses, and discussing news stories. The unit also developed opinion language using would rather.

What You Practised

You worked on describing people and situations.
Examples you practised:

They are good friends.
They are senior citizens.

You practised making guesses and giving opinions.
Examples:

Maybe they are meeting for the first time in many years.

You practised preferences using would rather.
Examples:

I’d rather be happy than sad.
I’d rather eat at home than in a restaurant.

You worked on relative clauses.
Examples:

Nancy is the senior citizen that Donna is helping.
The boy who’s wearing a blue T-shirt.

You practised talking about news stories.
Examples:

A teenager wins the lottery.
A woman gives a house to the homeless.

What to Practise More

1. Using would rather Naturally

Compare two clear options.

2. Relative Clauses Without Repetition

Use that or who smoothly.

Final Comment

This unit focused on preferences and description. To improve further, practise expressing opinions clearly and using relative clauses naturally.
