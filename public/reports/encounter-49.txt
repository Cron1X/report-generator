ncounter 49 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on environmental issues, causes and solutions, presentation language, and connecting past events to present results. The unit also developed opinion-based discussion skills.

What You Practised

You worked on environmental vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

pollution
toxic chemicals
oil spill
air pollution
water pollution

You practised discussing causes and solutions.
Examples:

There is too much rubbish.
People don’t recycle enough.
We should use public transport.

You practised presentation structure.
Examples:

Today I want to talk about…
Moving on, let’s think about…
Finally, I want to suggest…

You worked on why questions.
Examples:

Does anyone know why there is rubbish on the sidewalk?

You practised linking past events to present situations.
Examples:

There’s been an oil spill, and it’s causing problems.
The price of gas has increased, and now people drive less.

You practised ranking and discussing problems.
Examples:

Air pollution is the most serious problem.
Water pollution is less serious than air pollution.

What to Practise More

1. Explaining Cause and Effect

Use because and and now clearly.

2. Structuring Opinions

Use clear opening and closing phrases.

Final Comment

This unit focused on environmental issues and structured discussion. To improve further, practise explaining causes clearly and organizing ideas logically.
