Encounter 11 – Student Feedback Report

In this unit, we focused on using English in shopping and everyday situations. You worked on asking about prices, using a / an / the correctly, and describing places using there is and there are. You participated well and made a clear effort to use the target language during the speaking activities.

You practiced asking and answering questions in common shopping situations.
Examples:

How much is it?

How much does it cost?

Where can I buy it?

Can I try it on?

You were generally able to ask these questions clearly and respond appropriately.

Talking about prices and money was also an important focus.
Examples:

It’s three dollars.

It’s fifteen euros.

It costs five pounds.

You showed good understanding of how to give prices in full sentences.

You worked on using a, an, and the more accurately.
Examples:

a shirt

an apple

the store

the table

the cinema

At times, you needed to stop and think about which article to use, but you were able to correct yourself and communicate your meaning clearly.

You also practiced describing places using there is and there are.
Examples:

There is a chair in the room.

There are two tables.

Is there a supermarket near here?

Are there any chairs in the office?

There isn’t a café nearby.

There aren’t any seats.

You were able to choose the correct form in most cases and showed good awareness of singular and plural nouns.

To continue improving, focus on:

Choosing a / an / the more quickly and confidently

Using there is and there are without hesitation

Asking questions in full sentences every time

Overall, this unit helped you manage basic shopping conversations and describe places more clearly. With continued practice, your accuracy and confidence in everyday communication will continue to improve.
