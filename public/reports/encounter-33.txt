Encounter 33 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on sports vocabulary, making suggestions, talking about past activities, and expressing opinions about games and events.

What You Practised

You worked with sports vocabulary and equipment.
Examples you practised:

soccer
basketball
soccer ball
shorts
sneakers

It’s a soccer ball.
He’s kicking the ball.
He scores a goal.

You practised talking about games and scores.
Examples:

Brazil won.
It was 3–0.
Who won the game?
What was the score?

You worked on making suggestions.
Examples:

Let’s go to a soccer game this weekend.
We should go to a basketball game.
Why don’t we watch the game together?
How about going this weekend?

You practised talking about the past using the past simple.
Examples:

We went to a soccer game last weekend.
What did you do last weekend?
What did you do yesterday?

You practised giving opinions and reasons.
Examples:

I liked the game because it was exciting.
I didn’t like the game because it was boring.

What to Practise More

2. Giving Clear Opinions

Final Comment

This unit focused on sports, suggestions, and talking about past events. To improve further, practise past simple sentences and giving clear opinions with reasons.
