Encounter 59 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on types of homes, furniture, neighborhoods, and describing accommodation using relative clauses. The unit developed descriptive language for housing and lifestyle preferences.

What You Practised

You worked on vocabulary for buildings and surroundings.
Examples you practised:

apartment
mansion
townhouse
cottage
garden
fence
trees

You practised describing homes and their features.
Examples:

It’s a modern mansion.
It has a pool and a large garden.
There aren’t many trees.

You practised discussing advantages and disadvantages.
Examples:

It would be very quiet and peaceful.
Living in a village is relaxing.

You worked on relative clauses using who and which.
Examples:

He is the person who wants to buy my car.
My best friend Jack, who lives in New York, is a lawyer.
The house has a garden which has a fence around it.

You practised combining sentences for clearer descriptions.
Examples:

I live in an apartment which has three bedrooms.
The house has a garden which is very large.

You practised asking detailed questions about accommodation.
Examples:

Does it have a garden?
What is the neighborhood like?
How many rooms does it have?

What to Practise More

1. Relative Clauses

Practise avoiding repetition by using who and which naturally.

2. Descriptive Detail

Add more adjectives when describing homes.

Final Comment

This unit focused on describing homes clearly and accurately. To improve further, practise longer descriptions using relative clauses and varied adjectives.
