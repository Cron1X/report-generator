Encounter 52 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on household items, asking and telling people to do things, getting services done, and the passive voice. The unit also developed problem-solving language.

What You Practised

You worked on household vocabulary.
Examples you practised:

stool
sofa
chair
table

A stool is used to sit on.

You practised get / have something done.
Examples:

I’ll get it fixed.
I had my computer repaired.

You practised asking and telling people to do things.
Examples:

Could you clean my clothes?
I told you to cut the vegetables.

You worked on the passive voice.
Examples:

Has the coffee been made?
Yes, the coffee has been made.
The window has just been opened.

You practised problem-solving language.
Examples:

I’d call a repair shop.
I’d ask when it will be fixed.

What to Practise More

1. Passive Voice Focus

Emphasize the action, not the person.

2. Polite Requests

Use could you instead of commands.

Final Comment

This unit focused on household tasks, services, and polite requests. To improve further, practise passive forms and clear request language.
