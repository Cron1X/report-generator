Encounter 38 – Student Feedback Report

In this lesson, we focused on expressing feelings, likes and dislikes, making comparisons, and talking about experiences using ever and comparative structures.

What You Practised

You worked on vocabulary for feelings.
Examples you practised:

I feel excited.
I feel disappointed.
I feel delighted.
I’m not happy with it.

You practised expressing likes and dislikes strongly.
Examples:

I like Sofi.
I kind of like him.
I hate him.
I can’t stand him.

You worked on superlative and experience structures.
Examples:

The funniest person I’ve ever met.
The best movie I’ve ever seen.
It was the best restaurant I’ve ever been to.

You practised comparisons using as … as and not as … as.
Examples:

Shen is as tall as Benny.
Shen is not as tall as Benny.

You also practised talking about the best and worst experiences.
Examples:

What’s the best vacation you’ve ever had?
It’s better to stay here because it’s cheaper.

What to Practise More

1. Using ever with Superlatives

The best food I’ve ever eaten.
The worst movie I’ve ever seen.

2. Comparing Clearly

Not as interesting as
As good as

Final Comment

This unit focused on feelings, comparisons, and experiences. To improve further, practise superlatives with ever and comparing ideas clearly in full sentences.
